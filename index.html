To and Through College

The road to and through college is cluttered with obstacles – especially for students from under-resourced and overburdened communities. College Bound offers the support and guidance necessary to help first-generation students from low-income backgrounds get TO and THROUGH college.

MAKING A DIFFERENCE

College Bound started with two high schools and 36 students in 2006.Today we serve nearly 550 students from more than 30 area high schools.

92%

of students in CB's Class of 2022 matriculated to college within six months of graduation

47%

of current CB collegians are projected to have zero debt upon graduation

500%

more CB students earn degrees than their demographics would predict

“If I hadn’t joined CB, I don’t think I would have started or completed college. The application and financial aid process was a lot and it can be difficult to navigate. I received support with my NCLEX (nursing board) exam fees and the prep class fee that was added on at UMSL as a requirement last minute.”

Christion, CB Class of 2016

“There was never a time when I couldn’t reach out to my College Bound coaches – whether it was about academics or family. I didn’t have a father and I could reach out to my coaches for anything I needed. College Bound gave me the push I needed.”

Jeremiah, CB Class of 2011

“Coming to CB, you get a personalized coach. At first I didn’t know if I would click with someone like her, but she’s actually pretty dope. Claire gets it – she gets me.”

Bryona, CB@STLCC student

“The people here are so welcoming and understanding. They are so helpful. I came sophomore year and I was afraid to talk to anyone. I didn’t know if I could trust the people here. Now I know that I can put 100% of my trust in them because I know they will be there when I need them most. I feel safe here. I love College Bound.”

Alex, CB Class of 2020

“College Bound has helped me to think broadly, outside the box, deep into my future. I have learned so much and will take what I have learned with me to inspire others.”

Kiara, CB Class of 2020
